Paper Review: "Seeking Instructional Specificity"


  • In an intro physics course (N = 231), the authors tried two different ways to teach a physical analogy (contour maps and equipotential lines):
    • Asking students to create general rule (GR) from the analogy
    • Asking students to apply the analogy on a case-by-case (CC) basis
  • The GR group performed better on a post-test by ~10%.
  • Implications:
    • "scaffolded practice" should be interpreted with care.
    • In this study, [generating own rules] > [step-by-step guidance]
    • Many caveats from design of assessment questions, etc.
