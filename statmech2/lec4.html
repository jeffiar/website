Solving the 1D Ising Model

Game Plan

  1. Draw a picture to gain some physical intuition (we didn't do this in class!!)

  2. Rewrite the Hamiltonian as a sum over bonds (rather than sites AND bonds)

  3. Zoom in on a particular bond.

  4. Write down a transfer matrix which represents the bond from site to site

  5. Key step – Notice that summing over looks an awful lot like contracting over a shared index, a.k.a. matrix multiplication.

  6. Rewrite as the trace of a bunch of transfer matrices multiplied together.

  7. Similarly, rewrite the average spin and the correlation function in terms of transfer matrices.

Afterwards, we will diagonalize the transfer matrix, and we will use its eigenbasis to calculate a few interesting quantities. Throughout these steps, we'll appeal to Pauli matrices to help us calculate things, and we'll slowly build up an analogy with a quantum mechanical spin-half particle.
