Solving the 1D Ising Model

Now that we knew why we wanted to calculate certain things, we went on to explicitly solve the 1D Ising Model.

Remember that the first step is to compute the partition function, which requires us to sum over all the energy states. This is a bit of a hassle for us, since for a 1D Ising Model with sites, we need to sum over all the possible states. As we take the thermodynamic limit , there will just be too many terms to add up, so as Prof. Kivelson says, we'll need to be clever.

Here is our game plan:

Solving the 1D Ising Model by Being Clever

  1. Draw a picture to gain some physical intuition (we didn't do this in class!!)

  2. Rewrite the Hamiltonian as a sum over bonds (rather than sites AND bonds)

  3. Zoom in on a particular site and its neighboring bonds

  4. Write down a transfer matrix which represents site (or bond ? I haven't fully understood yet…)

  5. Key step – Notice that summing over looks an awful lot like contracting over a shared index, a.k.a. matrix multiplication.

  6. Rewrite the entire sum as the trace of a bunch of transfer matrices multiplied together.
