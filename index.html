I am an undergraduate studying physics at Stanford University. Broadly, I am intrigued by the physics of the biochemical world—the thermodynamics of life, the quantum chemistry of vision, the dynamical landscape of macromolecules, and more. In 2018, I did some research about the photophysics of fluorescent proteins in the Boxer lab.

Outside of science, I take piano lessons with Dr. Frederick Weldy, and I play horn in the Stanford Collaborative Orchestra (SCOr). I also play jazz piano in a trio called Casual Business.

On this website, you can learn some fascinating statistical mechanics and read about various scientific thoughts. You can enjoy some of my musical creations.
