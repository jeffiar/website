I am an undergraduate studying physics at Stanford University. Broadly, I am intrigued by the physics of the biochemical world -- the thermodynamics of life, the quantum chemistry of vision, the dynamical landscape of macromolecules, and more. I've done research about the photophysics of fluorescent proteins in the Boxer lab.

Outside of science, I take piano lessons with Dr. Weldy, and I play horn in the Stanford Collaborative Orchestra (SCOr). I also play jazz piano in a trio called Casual Business.

On my website, you can read about my various scientific thoughts, or learn some fascinating statistical mechanics from my lecture notes. You can also find my upcoming performances, recordings from past performances, and a catalogue of my musical creations.
