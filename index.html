I am an undergraduate studying physics at Stanford University. Broadly, I am intrigued by the physics of the biochemical world -- the thermodynamics of life, the quantum chemistry of vision, the dynamical landscape of macromolecules, and more. Currently, I'm doing research about the photophysics of fluorescent proteins in the Boxer lab.

I play horn in the Stanford Collaborative Orchestra, and I take piano lessons with Dr. Fred Weldy. In addition, I improvise music on the Stanford Improv team.

Here you can find my upcoming performances, recordings from past performances, and a catalogue of my musical creations. In addition, you can read about my various scientific thoughts, or learn some fascinating statistical mechanics from my lecture notes.
