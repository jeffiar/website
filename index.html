A bit about me: I want to become a scientist (whatever that means in 2021). Broadly, I am fascinated by the way that life works on the molecular to cellular level—how proteins perform their functions through random thermal jigglings; how they spontaneously organize themselves into larger structures; and how such complicated things manage to evolve in the first place. When I was an undergrad, I had the chance to do some research about the photophysics of fluorescent proteins in the Boxer lab at Stanford. Currently I am a first-year graduate student in the physics department at Harvard. I am fortunate to be supported by the NSF graduate fellowship. It is quite wonderful and humbling that our government is willing to support young people to pursue the natural sciences!
